
Description: Fits a linear degradation model to stability data and estimates shelf
             life as the time at which the confidence bound of the predicted mean
             crosses the specification limit. Uses the Brown-Forsythe homogeneity-
             of-variance test (robust to non-normal data).

Usage: jrc_shelf_life_linear <data.csv> <spec_limit> <confidence> [--direction low|high] [--transform log]

Arguments:
	data.csv       Two-column CSV with headers 'time' and 'value'.
	               One row per unit tested (pull-and-test / cross-sectional
	               design). Do NOT supply aggregated time-point means.
	               Individual measurements are required so that both within-
	               time-point and between-time-point variability are correctly
	               propagated into the confidence bounds.
	               Minimum: 4 observations across at least 3 time points.
	               Example: 6 time points x 3 units = 18 rows.
	spec_limit     The specification limit (numeric). The confidence bound of
	               the predicted mean must not cross this value.
	confidence     Confidence level, e.g. 0.95 for a 95% confidence bound.
	--direction    'low'  (default) — value must stay ABOVE spec_limit.
	               Use for degrading properties: peel strength, burst pressure,
	               tensile force, seal integrity.
	               'high' — value must stay BELOW spec_limit.
	               Use for growing properties: impurity level, particulate count.
	--transform    'log'  — fit lm(log(value) ~ time) instead of lm(value ~ time).
	               The confidence interval is back-transformed via exp() before
	               comparing to spec_limit, so results are on the original scale.
	               Use when residuals are right-skewed or when variance grows with
	               the mean (typical of log-normal data such as bioburden, moisture
	               uptake, or degradation byproduct counts). All values must be > 0.
	               'none' (default) — no transformation.
	-h|--help      Show this help message.

Method:
	Fits lm(value ~ time) on all individual measurements (not means).

	Homogeneity of variance test: Brown-Forsythe method (absolute deviations
	from group medians), equivalent to car::leveneTest with center=median.
	This test is robust to non-normal distributions — safe to use on skewed
	data (unlike Bartlett's test). Alpha = 0.05.

	Shelf life = time t* where the lower (or upper) confidence bound of
	the predicted mean E[value|t*] equals spec_limit. Solved numerically
	using uniroot(). The confidence interval on the MEAN (not a prediction
	interval for a new observation) is used, consistent with ICH Q1E.

Output:
	Terminal: homogeneity test, regression summary, shelf life estimate.
	~/Downloads/{timestamp}_jrc_shelf_life_linear.png — plot showing scatter,
	  regression line, confidence band, spec limit, and shelf life marker.
	~/Downloads/{timestamp}_jrc_shelf_life_linear_model.csv — model coefficient
	  file for use with jrc_shelf_life_extrapolate.

Notes:
	If the confidence bound at the first time point already violates the
	spec limit, the script exits with an error. This indicates a product
	that does not meet specification even at the start of the study.

	A non-significant slope (p >= 0.05) means the data do not confirm
	a trend over time. The shelf life estimate is reported with a warning.
	Consider collecting more data or extending the time range.

	The regression line is shown solid within the observed time range
	and dashed beyond it (extrapolated region) on the plot.

Example:
	jrc_shelf_life_linear stability_data.csv 80.0 0.95

	Fits a linear model to data in stability_data.csv, estimates the time
	at which the lower 95% confidence bound crosses 80.0.

	jrc_shelf_life_linear stability_data.csv 80.0 0.95 --transform log

	Fits a log-linear model (lm on log(value)); back-transforms the CI
	before finding when it crosses 80.0. Use for right-skewed data.

	jrc_shelf_life_linear impurity_data.csv 0.10 0.95 --direction high

	Estimates when the upper 95% CI bound for an impurity exceeds 0.10.

Reference:
	ICH Q1E, Evaluation for Stability Data, 2003.
	Section 4.1: shelf life estimate from the intersection of the confidence
	interval on the mean with the acceptance criterion.
