Description: Determines the minimum number of paired observations needed to detect a
             specified difference between two conditions using a paired t-test. Results
             are shown as a table over standard combinations of power and confidence.

Usage: jrc_ss_paired "delta" "sd" "sides"

Arguments:
       delta   the minimum meaningful difference to detect between the two conditions
               (absolute value, same units as the measurement). This is the maximum
               allowable difference specified in the verification protocol.
               Must be a positive number.
       sd      expected standard deviation of the paired differences. Estimate from
               a pilot study or prior paired data. Must be a positive number.
               Note: the SD of paired differences is typically smaller than the SD
               of individual measurements because between-unit variability cancels.
       sides   1 for a 1-sided test (direction of difference is pre-specified)
               2 for a 2-sided test (direction of difference is unknown)
       -h| --help  Show this help message

Output:
       Reports a table of minimum number of pairs for power = 0.90, 0.95, 0.99
       and confidence = 0.90, 0.95, 0.99. Also highlights the FDA-recommended
       combination (power = 0.95, confidence = 0.95).

       Note: n is the number of pairs, not the total number of observations. Each
       pair consists of one measurement per condition on the same unit or subject.

       1-sided test: use only when the direction of the difference is pre-specified
       in the protocol. Post-hoc selection of 1-sided testing is not acceptable.

Example:
       jrc_ss_paired "0.5" "1.0" "2"
       jrc_ss_paired "0.5" "1.0" "1"
